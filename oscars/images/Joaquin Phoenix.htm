Exclusive: This was Joaquin Phoenix's secret grooming weapon at the Oscars 

Groomer David Cox shows us what it takes to get Oscar Awards-worthy skin

By Zak Maoui

10 February 2020

    Joaquin Phoenix won big at the 2020 Oscars, scooping (unsurprisingly) the Best Actor award thanks to his impressive turn as the Joker, in the film of the same name. And being a guest, let alone an Oscar nominee, at the world's most prestigious awards ceremony calls for three things - an impeccable outfit that will make best-dressed lists the world over, a speech that will get people talking and a grooming regime that will cover up the results of those nerve-fuelled sleepless nights. 

    And Phoenix secured all three. Wearing the same Stella McCartney suit that he wore to the BAFTAs in a sustainable move, and giving us one of the best injustice-fighting Oscar speeches in history, it was a sweepstake for him, especially with regards to his grooming regime.

    With the help of David Cox, the celebrity groomer who has worked with the likes of Jared Leto and Pierce Brosnan, as well as Phoenix, the Oscar-winning actor was literally glowing. And the secret? Make-up. “For the Oscars Joaquin likes his skin to look very natural with as little fuss as possible" Cox tells us, “He naturally has very good skin so he does not need a foundation, but only a moisturiser and a good smooth textured concealer to cover any blemishes and dark areas. I like the concealer to have a nice smooth finish and great coverage without taking all his natural blemishes away, I like to soften them.”

    “I use 3.0 and a small amount of 4.0 EX1 Cosmetics Delete Fluide Concealer as it gives my application a perfect colour match and a nice smooth moisturised finish. To finish the look I use this great powder, EX1 Invisiwear in 4.0 which perfectly compliments the concealer and leaves the skin with a perfect sheen.”

    And it's no surprise, that the secret weapon to Phoenix's Oscar-winning look is make-up (a £10.50 concealer would you believe). More and more men are getting on board (albeit being extremely late to the party), from Harry Styles to Ansel Elgort (in eye shadow nonetheless), and we even had a category dedicated to it in the last GQ Grooming Awards. 

    And Joaquin Phoenix, is the man showing everyone at home how they can use make-up, without looking like a contestant on RuPaul's Drag Race or indeed, Phoenix's on-screen Joker. The key is to find, as Phoenix and Cox have, a perfect matching concealer, that will literally hide bags, blemishes and imperfections in one application. With more and more brands putting out male-dedicated make-up lines, from Chanel (a new drop is coming FYI) to Tom Ford, it's only getting easier for us to wear it, without being critiqued.

    As our Style and Grooming Director Teo van den Broeke puts it, “For the first time ever, it might actually be legitimately possible for men, straight men – who enjoy sports and date their high school sweethearts – to really, truly get away with wearing nail varnish, foundation, lipstick and, well, glittery eye shadow and, perhaps most importantly, look great doing it.”

    So gents, get yourself down to Superdrug this lunch time, because the days of the under-eye bags are over.
